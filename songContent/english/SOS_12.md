# Song 1:8-17 In Love

1. If you do not know, most beautiful one,
Follow the trail of the flock, of the flock,
And feed your young goats, feed your little ones,
Beside the tents of the shepherds.
O’ my darling, you are like my mare
Among the chariots of Pharaoh.
Your cheeks are lovely with ornaments,
Your neck with strings of jewels.
We’ll make you jewe’ry of gold, with silver.

2. My perfume gave forth, gave forth its fragrance
While the king was at his table.
A poach of myrrh is my beloved to me
That lies all night between my breasts.
My beloved is a cluster of henna
In the vineyards of Engedi.
How beautiful You are, my darling!
Your eyes are doves, O’ my sister!
Your eyes are doves, O’ my sister!
How handsome you are,
My beloved! You're truly pleasant!
And our bed is green, it is green;
The beams of our house are cedar;
The rafters are pine, they are pine.
