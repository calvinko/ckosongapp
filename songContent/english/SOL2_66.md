# The Lord's Sorrow and His Beloved in the Garden

My dear Lord faced the power of darkness,
He alone foretast'd the cup.
In the garden so grieved, He prayed in tears.
His sweats were like drops of blood.

2. The Lord found His disciples asleep.
They could not watch for a while.
"The spirit is willing; the flesh is weak,"
He said with sympathy.

3. The Lord found His disciples asleep.
He did not blame nor complain.
With love, He urged them to watch and pray,
Lest they enter temptations.

4. He found them weary and asleep again.
He was driven to die for them.
He looked upon them with compassion.
His heart was deeply moved.

5. He longed for them to be with Him fore'er,
Sit on the throne with them.
He treasured their friendship in those three years,
The days of laughter and tears.

6. How could He not give Himself for them?
How could He not drink the cup?
He's willing. Eagerly He prayed in tears,
"Father, may Your will be done!"

7. O Lord, I am amazed at Your love.
I'm Your drive to go to the cross.
I'll always think of Your lifeyielding love.
May I love You only.

8. All the days of my life I'll consider
My favoured position in You.
May I be mindful of Your gaze of love,
Feel deeply Your love for me.
