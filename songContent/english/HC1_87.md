# Song 4:7-16 Come Along With Me, Darling

O My darling, you are so beautiful!
There is no blemish in you!
May you come along with Me, My bride,
Come with Me from Lebanon.
May you come with Me from Lebanon;
From the dens of lions, and mountains of leopards;
Journey down from the peak. My sister, My bride,
You've made My heart beat fast with a glance of your eyes;
A strand of your necklace steals My heart.

O My darling, My sister, My bride,
How beautiful is your love!
How much better is your love than wine;
And the fragrance of your oils
Is better than all kinds of spices.
My sister, My bride, you are a garden locked
A rock garden locked, a spring sealed up.
You are a garden spring, a well of fresh water,
And streams flowing from Lebanon.

Awake, north wind, and come, wind of the south,
Blow into my garden!
Make my garden breathe out fragrance;
May my Beloved come and eat
All kinds of choice fruits in His garden.
