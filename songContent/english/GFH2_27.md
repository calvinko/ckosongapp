# The Steadfast Love

Is there any love as Lord Your love,
More lasting than the universe?
Through all the ages Your thoughts for me
Outnumber the sand of the sea.
Hark, the voice of my Beloved!
Behold, from heaven He came.
To find me He came from heaven.
And took the form of a slave.
Why for me a homeless life You lived,
Acquaint’d with pain and grief?
I retrace in Bethany
Before the tomb You did weep.

God’s love is the same to the end.
Behold the perfect Son of Man,
Without a trace of changing shadow,
He’s gentle and mild even now.
His gaze is tender and charming,
Draws me from wandering.
It melts my heart, it melts my heart,
It melts my unyielding heart.
Why You had a marred and grieved form,
No place to lay Your head?
I retrace on the cruel cross
Your love that overcame death.

I have to give thanks, I am amazed,
Such love in this world I’ve obtained.
To me steadfast and unchanging,
It’s You who’s ever-loving.
Your love that great waters cannot quench,
Deserves all my heart to gain.
