# Song 2:14-17

1. O’ my dove, in the clefts of the rock,
In the secret places of the cliff,
Let me see your face and hear your voice;
Let me see your face, let me hear your voice,
For your voice is sweet to me
And your face is lovely.
Catch the foxes for us, for us,
The little foxes that spoil the vineyards,
For our vineyards are in blossom, in blossom

2. My beloved is mine and I am his.
My beloved pastures his flock;
He feeds his flock among the lilies.
My beloved, until the cool of the day,
When the shadows flee away,
Turn my beloved, turn my beloved,
Like a gazelle, like a young stag,
On the mountains, the mountains of Bether,
A young stag on the mountains of Bether.
