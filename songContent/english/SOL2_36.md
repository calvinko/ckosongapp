# Enjoy the Lord (Song 5:9-6:1)

O' most beautiful among women,
What is your beloved more than other beloved,
What is your beloved more than other beloved,
O' fairest one, that you so charge us?

My beloved's dazzling and ruddy,
Outstanding among ten thousand.
His head is the finest gold, pure gold;
His locks are wavy, his locks are wavy,
Black as a raven.
His eyes as doves by the rivers,
With milk, they're washed and fitly set.
A bed of spices, 
banks of scented herbs are his cheeks.
His lips are like lilies,
Dripping liquid myrrh.
His hands are like the rods of gold
And set with beryl.
His body is as carved ivory,
Inlaid with sapphires.
His legs, pillars of marble,
Set on bases of fine gold.
His bearing as Lebanon,
Excellent as the cedars.
His mouth is full of sweetness,
He's altogether lovely.
O' daughters of Jerusalem,
This is my beloved and this is my friend.

O' most beautiful among women,
Where about has your beloved gone?
Where has your beloved turned aside?
We will seek him together with you,
We will seek him together with you.
