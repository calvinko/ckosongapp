# Gain Christ

I was lost in worldly pleasures,
And indulged in mortal things;
Strayed from "Fount' of Living Water,"
Bound in sin, how mis'rable!
Now, the glory of Christ draws me,
My heart's melted in His great love;
Forget things behind, reach forward,
Press toward the goal ahead.

2. All things that are under the sun,
Are vanity, toils in vain.
Soon the world, its lusts will vanish.
As the mist, none will remain.
The Lord alone will never change.
Only He satisfies my heart.
Whom have I in heaven but Christ?
I desire nothing on earth.

3. Whatever things were gain to me,
All I have counted as loss
For knowing Christ Jesus my Lord,
My greatest treasure of all.
For whom I have suffered the loss
Of all things, and count'd them as dross,
That I may gain Christ my Lord,
That I may gain Christ my Lord.
